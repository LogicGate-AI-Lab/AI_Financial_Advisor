## 2. Key News Details
*Summaries of the day's most important finance, market, economic, and macroeconomic news.*

1.  **Iran War Tightens Tech Supply Chains via Helium Disruption:** Iran's attacks have damaged Qatar's natural gas infrastructure, halting production of helium, a critical byproduct used in semiconductor manufacturing, medical imaging, and aerospace. Qatar supplies one-third of the world's helium, posing a significant risk to advanced industries. [8]
2.  **U.S. Temporarily Lifts Sanctions on Iranian Oil:** The U.S. Treasury authorized a short-term license for the purchase of 140 million barrels of Iranian crude already at sea. The move aims to alleviate soaring global oil prices exacerbated by the war but may provide revenue to Iran. [14, 35]
3.  **Trump Sends Mixed Signals on Middle East Military Campaign:** President Trump suggested the U.S. was considering "winding down" military efforts in Iran, even as the Pentagon dispatches 2,200 more Marines and three naval ships to the region. He also issued a 48-hour ultimatum for Iran to open the Strait of Hormuz or face strikes on its power plants. [6, 30, 48, 53, 69]
4.  **U.S. Homeland Security Funding Impasse Causes Major Airport Delays:** A partial government shutdown has left TSA workers unpaid, leading to severe staff shortages, security line wait times exceeding three hours at major airports, and widespread flight disruptions. A Senate bill to fund the Department of Homeland Security failed to advance. [11, 28, 51, 73]
5.  **Jury Finds Elon Musk Liable for Misleading Twitter Investors:** A federal jury determined that Elon Musk's 2022 tweets about the Twitter acquisition artificially depressed the company's stock price, misleading investors. He was found liable but cleared of fraud allegations. Damages could reach billions. [32, 33, 45]
6.  **Georgia Becomes First State to Suspend Fuel Tax:** Governor Brian Kemp signed a law suspending Georgia's gas and diesel taxes for 60 days in response to soaring fuel prices linked to the Middle East conflict. Other states, however, appear unlikely to follow due to tighter fiscal conditions. [68]
7.  **UK Borrowing Costs Hit 18-Year High Amid War Fallout:** A report suggests the war in Iran has pushed UK government borrowing costs to an 18-year high, threatening the Chancellor's economic plans and potentially necessitating new tax hikes. [16]
8.  **Federal Judge Strikes Down Pentagon Press Policy:** A U.S. district judge ruled that a restrictive Pentagon press credentialing policy implemented under Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth is unconstitutional, violating First and Fifth Amendment rights. The policy had led to an exodus of major news organizations from the Pentagon briefing room. [22, 40, 46, 47]
9.  **White House Expands TrumpRx Drug Discount Platform:** The administration added three drugs for diabetes and lung disease to its self-pay prescription drug platform, touting significant discounts. Critics note the platform's limited scope and the availability of cheaper generics for some medications. [43]
10. **Prediction Market Kalshi Temporarily Banned in Nevada:** A Nevada judge issued a 14-day restraining order against prediction market platform Kalshi, barring it from offering contracts on sports, elections, and entertainment in the state without a gaming license. [67]
11. **CBS News Shutters Historic Radio News Service:** As part of a round of layoffs under editor-in-chief Bari Weiss, CBS News is closing its nearly century-old radio news service, which provided content to approximately 700 affiliate stations. [70, 71]
12. **China Approves First Commercial Brain-Computer Interface Chip:** Chinese regulators approved a commercially available brain implant from Neuracle Medical Technology designed to help paralyzed patients control robotic limbs, marking a milestone in the race to dominate the neurotech industry. [75]
